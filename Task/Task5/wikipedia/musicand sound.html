MUSIC AND SOUND

Music and Sound in cinema refer to all the audio elements used in a film — including background music, dialogues, sound effects, and silence — to create mood, emotion, and realism. Sound is just as important as visuals. While the camera shows what we see, sound helps us feel and connect with the story.

Types of Music in Films

  • Background Score (BGM)
    Instrumental music that plays behind scenes to match the emotion. Example: Intense BGM during a fight or chase.
  • Songs / Soundtracks
    Used for entertainment or to express feelings through lyrics. Example: Romantic songs, dance numbers, montage songs.
  • Theme Music
    Repeated tune that represents a character, place, or emotion. Example: “Vikram” or “K.G.F.” hero entry music.

    • Stages of Sound Production

  • Recording – Capturing dialogues and ambient sounds during shooting.
  • Sound Design – Creating and adding extra effects (explosions, footsteps).
  • Music Composition – Background music and songs are composed.
  • Dubbing – Re-recording dialogues clearly in the studio.
  • Mixing and Mastering – Combining all sounds (dialogue, music, SFX) at proper volume levels.
