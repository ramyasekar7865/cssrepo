Samuthirakani

Samuthirakani is a well-known actor, director, and writer in Tamil cinema, admired for his realistic performances and socially responsible films. He was born on April 26, 1973, in Seithur, Rajapalayam, Tamil Nadu. After completing his education, he moved to Chennai to pursue a career in cinema. He started his journey by assisting the famous director K. Balachander, which helped him gain valuable experience in filmmaking. Samuthirakani made his directorial debut with Unnai Charanadainthen (2003) and earned recognition for directing meaningful and message-oriented movies like Naadodigal, Poraali, Appa, and Vinodhaya Sitham. His films often focus on human values, education, and social equality, making him one of the most respected filmmakers in Tamil cinema.

As an actor, Samuthirakani is known for his natural acting style and powerful dialogue delivery. He has played important roles in both Tamil and Telugu films, including Subramaniapuram, Easan, Velaiyilla Pattathari, Vikram, and Sir (Vaathi). He won the National Film Award for Best Supporting Actor for the movie Visaranai (2016), directed by Vetrimaaran. Apart from acting, he continues to direct and write films that highlight social issues and inspire change. With his dedication, simplicity, and commitment to meaningful cinema, Samuthirakani has become a symbol of honest storytelling and strong moral values in the Indian film industry.
