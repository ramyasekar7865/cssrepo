Prakash Raj

Prakash Raj is a highly acclaimed Indian actor, director, producer, and television presenter known for his versatility and commanding screen presence across multiple Indian film industries, including Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Hindi cinema. Born on March 26, 1965, in Bengaluru, Karnataka, he began his career in Kannada theatre and television before transitioning to films. He gained national recognition for his ability to portray a wide range of roles — from intense villains and strict fathers to compassionate mentors and comic characters. His breakthrough came with films like Iruvar (1997) directed by Mani Ratnam and Antahpuram (1998), which established him as one of the most powerful performers in Indian cinema.

Apart from acting, Prakash Raj is also a successful filmmaker and producer, known for backing meaningful and socially relevant cinema through his production house “Duet Movies.” He has received numerous awards, including multiple National Film Awards, for both acting and production. Fluent in several languages, he is celebrated for bringing depth, realism, and emotional intensity to his characters. Beyond films, Prakash Raj is known for his outspoken views on social and political issues, making him not just a cinematic icon but also a prominent public voice in India.
