Saranya Ponvannan

Saranya Ponvannan is a talented and versatile Indian actress known for her natural performances and motherly roles in Tamil cinema. She was born on April 25, 1970, in Alappuzha, Kerala, and made her acting debut in the film Nayagan (1987), directed by Mani Ratnam, opposite Kamal Haasan. After a few films as a heroine in the late 1980s and early 1990s, she took a break from acting and later returned to cinema in strong supporting roles. Her comeback was highly successful, and she became one of the most popular actresses known for playing mother characters with warmth, emotion, and depth.

Saranya has acted in many hit films such as Raam, Thavamai Thavamirundhu, Em Magan, Vel, Bangalore Naatkal, Velaiyilla Pattathari (VIP), and 24. She has also appeared in several Malayalam, Telugu, and Kannada films. Her performance in Thenmerku Paruvakaatru (2010) won her the National Film Award for Best Actress. Known for her expressive acting, simplicity, and strong screen presence, Saranya Ponvannan continues to be a beloved figure in South Indian cinema. She is respected for bringing realism and emotional power to her roles, making every character she plays memorable and relatable to audiences.
