Nagesh

Nagesh was one of the greatest comedy and character actors in the history of Tamil cinema. He was born as C. Krishna Rao Gundu Rao on September 27, 1933, in Dharapuram, Tamil Nadu. Nagesh began his career as a stage artist and later entered films in the 1950s. His first major success came with the movie Server Sundaram (1964), in which he played the lead role of a hotel waiter who dreams of becoming a successful actor. The film became a classic and showcased Nagesh’s extraordinary acting skills, blending humor with deep emotion.

Nagesh appeared in more than 1,000 films over five decades, working with legendary actors like M.G. Ramachandran (MGR), Sivaji Ganesan, Kamal Haasan, and Rajinikanth. He was known for his perfect comic timing, expressive face, and ability to perform both comedy and serious roles with equal excellence. Some of his memorable films include Ethir Neechal, Kadhalikka Neramillai, Thiruvilaiyadal, Michael Madana Kamarajan, and Apoorva Sagodharargal.

Nagesh received many awards and honors for his contribution to cinema, and he is often referred to as the “Charlie Chaplin of South India.” His dedication, talent, and versatility made him a legendary figure who inspired generations of actors. Nagesh passed away on January 31, 2009, but his legacy continues to live on through his timeless performances and unforgettable characters.
