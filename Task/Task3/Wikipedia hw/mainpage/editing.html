EDITING

Editing in cinema means arranging and joining together the shots and scenes of a film to create a smooth, meaningful, and emotionally effective story. It is the final stage of filmmaking where the director and editor work together to shape the movie’s rhythm, flow, and emotion.

Purpose of Editing

  • Tell the story clearly — by arranging scenes in the correct order.
  • Maintain continuity — so that time, place, and action flow naturally.
  • Create emotion and mood — using the right timing, music, and visual cuts.
  • Remove unwanted shots — and keep only the best takes.
  • Add effects and transitions — for smooth visual flow (fade, dissolve, wipe, etc.).

    • Stages of Film Editing

  • Selection of Shots
    The editor receives many hours of raw footage. The best shots are selected based on acting, lighting, and clarity.
  • Rough Cut
    Shots are arranged in the correct sequence (scene order) as per the script. No effects or sound corrections yet — only basic structure.
  • Fine Cut
    Timing of each shot is adjusted. Dialogue, reaction shots, and emotions are balanced. Rhythm of the film is created here.
  • Final Cut (Picture Lock)
    The film is completely shaped — no more changes in visuals. Then music, sound effects, and color correction are added.
