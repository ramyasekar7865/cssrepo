Vivek

Vivek was a highly respected comedian, actor, and social activist in Tamil cinema, admired for his intelligent humor and meaningful dialogues. He was born on November 19, 1961, in Kovai (Coimbatore), Tamil Nadu. After completing his studies, he entered the film industry with the help of veteran director K. Balachander, who introduced him to Tamil cinema. Vivek’s style of comedy was unique — he used humor not just to make people laugh but also to spread social awareness on topics like education, environment, women’s rights, and superstition.

He became famous through films like Run, Saamy, Minnale, Anniyan, Sivaji, and Dhil. His dialogues were sharp, witty, and educational, often carrying a strong moral message. Vivek’s comedy always had a blend of laughter and learning, earning him the title “Chinna Kalaivanar”, meaning “Little Kalaivanar,” as a tribute to the great comedian N. S. Krishnan.

Apart from acting, Vivek was also a social worker and an environmentalist. Inspired by former President Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, he started the “Green Kalam” project, through which he planted millions of trees across Tamil Nadu. He won several Tamil Nadu State Film Awards and the Padma Shri award from the Government of India for his contribution to cinema and society. Vivek passed away on April 17, 2021, but his legacy continues to live on through his inspiring comedy and social work.
