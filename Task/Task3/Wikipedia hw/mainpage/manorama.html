Manorama

Manorama, popularly known as “Aachi Manorama”, was one of the most celebrated and versatile actresses in Tamil cinema. She was born as Gopishantha on May 26, 1937, in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. Manorama began her career as a stage artist and later entered films with Maalaiyitta Mangai (1958). Over the next six decades, she became one of the most loved and respected actresses in South Indian cinema. Known for her comedy timing, strong dialogue delivery, and expressive acting, Manorama appeared in more than 1,500 films and 1,000 stage plays, earning a place in the Guinness World Records.

Throughout her career, she received numerous awards, including the Padma Shri and several Tamil Nadu State Film Awards. She was affectionately called “Aachi” by her fans for her loving and motherly screen presence. Manorama passed away on October 10, 2015, but her contribution to Indian cinema remains timeless. She will always be remembered as a legendary actress who brought life, laughter, and emotion to Tamil films.

She worked with almost all leading actors and directors, including M.G. Ramachandran, Sivaji Ganesan, Rajinikanth, Kamal Haasan, and Vijay. Her performances in films like Ethir Neechal, Anbe Vaa, Nayakan, Chinna Gounder, Samsaram Adhu Minsaram, and Indian are unforgettable. Manorama was equally brilliant in comedy and emotional roles, making audiences laugh and cry with ease.
